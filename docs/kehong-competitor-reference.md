# Kehong Competitor Reference Library

This document turns the user-provided competitor notes into a reusable design and conversion reference for future Kehong site work. Use it as a product/design brief before changing home, product, factory, inquiry, and 3D configurator sections.

## Strategic Target

Kehong should not copy traditional packaging factory sites directly. Most competitors are functional but visually conservative. Kehong should keep the proven B2B conversion structure while looking more premium, interactive, and easier to inquire from.

Primary positioning:

- Factory-backed paper packaging material supplier.
- Custom corrugated, specialty paper, food packaging, gift/display boxes, and protective packaging.
- Overseas lead generation first, domestic credibility second.
- Premium enough for brand buyers, practical enough for factories and distributors.

## Common Competitor Structure

Most competitor sites follow this sequence:

1. Hero with factory, product, or automated equipment imagery.
2. One-sentence custom packaging capability statement.
3. Product categories, usually 4 to 8 groups.
4. Factory strength: area, equipment, years, certifications.
5. OEM/ODM capability and process flow.
6. Product cards with image, material, MOQ, usage, and quote button.
7. Inquiry CTA repeated across homepage, product pages, and footer.

The conversion logic is:

Show factory -> show capability -> show product fit -> push inquiry.

## Kehong Differentiation Opportunities

Most competitors are weak in these areas:

- Visual design feels generic or industrial.
- Product inquiry path is often present but not persuasive.
- Few sites make factory content feel cinematic or interactive.
- Product specs are shown, but buyers still need help selecting options.
- Case studies and real application storytelling are often thin.
- 3D or interactive packaging structure visualization is rare.

Kehong should win by combining:

- Real product/factory images.
- Stronger interactive product exploration.
- Clear material/process/spec guidance.
- Repeated quote actions.
- A more premium design language than normal factory websites.

## Homepage Execution Direction

Required modules:

1. Cinematic hero using real product/factory imagery.
2. Product families: food packaging, display/gift boxes, corrugated/specialty paper, protective materials.
3. Interactive 3D Product Studio for key products.
4. Bakery packaging focus: cake boxes, kraft/window boxes, biodegradable options, cupcake inserts.
5. Factory capability band: years, area, equipment, sampling, quality.
6. Process flow: raw paper -> printing/lamination -> die-cut/creasing -> forming -> QC/packing.
7. Sustainability/custom capability section.
8. Featured SKU/catalog entry.
9. Inquiry section with WhatsApp and quote form.

Avoid:

- Purely decorative gradients.
- Generic factory copy without proof.
- Landing-page hero that does not show product or factory.
- Hiding inquiry buttons at the bottom only.

## Product Page Execution Direction

Every product detail page should eventually include:

- Product images or application images.
- SKU and product family.
- Material, GSM/thickness, structure/flute, surface process.
- Common size or custom size note.
- MOQ and sample/lead-time note where available.
- Application scenarios.
- Custom options: color, print, lamination, embossing, foil, die-cut, food liner.
- Strong inquiry button near top and after spec table.
- Related products.

Bakery product pages should additionally show:

- Cake height/diameter fit.
- Window or no-window version.
- Food liner and greaseproof options.
- Cupcake tray or inner insert fit.
- Flat-pack or pre-formed delivery format.
- Wholesale quantity bands and sample path.

## 3D Studio Direction

Keep improving the interactive product studio around buyer decision-making:

- Pizza box: opening angle, food liner, flute layer, print panel, finish point.
- Honeycomb paper: roll, expanded honeycomb mesh, cushioning use case.
- Display box: front display, insert tray, premium finish, retail shelf context.
- Scene presets should switch product, material, finish, quantity, and hotspot.
- Scroll must always work over the model area.
- Drag rotation should remain available.
- Visual background should use real materials/factory imagery, not flat color.
